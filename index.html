Biomechanics

Explore optimal hand and finger positioning. Flattened thumb or pinky can cause tension leading to shaky aim. Learn to keep both sides balanced.

  • Relaxed pinky and thumb for smooth left/right motion.
  • Elbow ~90°, shoulder loose to avoid transferring tension down to the wrist.
  • Find a grip style (palm/claw/fingertip) that supports easy pivot.

Attention & Reintegration

Rather than hyper-focusing on your wrist or the enemy’s position, anchor your awareness in a wide-field visual state. This allows faster “re-binding” after any shock (flash, bump, etc.).

  1. Wide-Field Vision: Keep the entire screen in view, using peripheral cues.
  2. Neural Coherence: A steady top-down rhythm helps your motor system self-correct smoothly.
  3. Quick Recovery: If you tense up, re-center visually and let the posture follow automatically.

Practice Drills

Structured routines to build muscle memory without reinforcing bad habits.

Warmup Routine

Spend 5 minutes on posture checks. Gently curl pinky and thumb. Practice wide-field scanning in an aim trainer (e.g. KovaaK’s or Aim Lab).

Multi-Target Flicks

Set up a scenario with multiple sporadic targets. Keep your gaze broad; let the crosshair move where your peripheral vision senses a target.

Chaos Recovery

Simulate random flashes or obstacles. Immediately reset to wide-field upon recovery and notice how quickly your aim stabilizes.
